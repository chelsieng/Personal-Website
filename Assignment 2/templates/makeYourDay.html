Tribute to All Broken Souls

Dear reader, my sincere greetings. Today I am about to show you a side I have always hidden. Why now? Simply because sometimes people write what they cannot say. Overall, my life has never been peaceful: it was basically an endless cycle of hard times and obstacles that no one could look upon. However, presently, I can proudly announce that I am a survivor who never gave up and a warrior, still fighting against the struggle of life. To me, writing this essay means pouring my heart out, revealing my true identity and sharing what I learnt through these hardships.

First, I will take you back to five years ago, the most painful and changing phase of my life. Ijeoma Umebinyo once wrote, “so many broken children living in grown bodies mimicking adult lives.” (Rip Robin Williams and many more, pinterest.com) In fact, I am one of those children. I was only twelve when my father was arrested. Indeed, a little girl’s biggest fan and so-called hero of her world had been snatched. Not only did she lose her father, she also felt like she did not belong here anymore. All her hopes and dreams were shattered, she felt her world crashing down and fell into complete darkness.

As my twelve-year-old self, I barely understood what was happening but what I did acknowledge was the permanent series of unfortunate events ahead of me. I grew up too fast: I failed in making the most of my childhood. I felt intense emotions prematurely. I remember drastically shifting from a super confident, spoiled little princess to someone full of fears and insecurities. All eyes were on me and I constantly felt judged. Every night, I would cry myself to sleep. I would visualize the most traumatic and heartbreaking scene of my father being savagely taken away handcuffed. Every thought about him made my heart heavy. Farewell to whims, to daddy-daughter moments, to a happily ever after family. Farewell to a father who would never see his daughter grow again. I was just a broken girl frustrated at how life had struck at her so harshly that she envied others’ happiness.

Nevertheless, as cliché as it may sound, I found aglow flickering in my darkest days. Welcome to the enlivening phase of my life. I dedicate this phase to all my dearest friends. Without them I would not be who I am now: cheerful, strong and fearless. They made life worth living again. They were my happiness, my strength and my motivation. My friends were the heroes of my miserable life. They would say, “Chelsie, you’ve been through so much and yet you’re so joyful and smiling. You’re such an inspiration, you truly command respect and I’ll always admire you.” Those words will forever be engraved in my heart.

Last, but not least, here is my realization phase. Pain is an inevitable part of life. We do not choose the events obstructing our way and I believe one should see the beauty of it. Pain has deeply shaped me into becoming a better person. It changed my perception of life, values and goals. I learned to cherish little things, the good days through the bad and I learned to see the best in everyone. I feel empathy for anyone because I know that behind every genuine smile lies an untold story. We should all remember, one never loses: one either wins or learn.

Today, I am here as a winner. Getting into university means the world to me. It will bring me one step ahead to attain a stable life and to finally feel at home again. Despite having a happy personality and a broken soul in one body, I am thankful for my scars. I now believe I am ready to face and fight the true reality of life. Someday, I hope to inspire others as much as I inspired my beloved, showing them that there will always be a way up and giving up is never an option. After all no one should be ashamed of their story. “Your experience is everything. Your lessons, mistakes, highs and lows, they all make up who you are.” (thegoodquote, instagram.com)
